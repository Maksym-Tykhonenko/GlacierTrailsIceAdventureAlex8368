About
Perito Moreno is one of the most famous glaciers in Patagonia and one of the most visited natural landmarks in Argentina. 21. Hubbard Glacier
Coordinates: 60.0167, -139.5000
Type: Tidewater Glacier
Location: Alaska, USA / Yukon, Canada
Status: Advancing / Surging in Some Periods
Area: 3,900 km²
Elevation: 3,400 m
Temp: -9°C
About
Hubbard Glacier is one of the most impressive tidewater glaciers in North America. It begins high in the mountains near the border of Alaska and Yukon, then flows toward Disenchantment Bay and Russell Fjord. Unlike many glaciers that are retreating, Hubbard has been known for periods of advance, which makes it especially interesting for glacier observation and scientific study.
Its enormous ice front rises above the water and often produces powerful calving events. When large blocks of ice break away, they fall into the bay with a deep sound and create waves across the cold water. Because the glacier reaches the ocean, it constantly interacts with tides, seawater, storms, and floating ice. This makes Hubbard Glacier a strong example of how land ice and ocean systems are connected.
Key Facts
One of the largest tidewater glaciers in North America
Flows from the Saint Elias Mountains toward the sea
Known for dramatic calving into Disenchantment Bay
Has shown periods of advance instead of constant retreat
Can temporarily block parts of Russell Fjord during major advances
Important for studying tidewater glacier behavior
Popular viewing location for cruise routes in Alaska
Its ice front can be several kilometers wide

Location: Ilulissat, Greenland
Status: Rapidly Changing

About
Jakobshavn Glacier, also known as Sermeq Kujalleq, is one of Greenland’s most famous and scientifically important glaciers. It drains ice from the Greenland Ice Sheet into Ilulissat Icefjord, where huge icebergs break away and drift toward the sea. The icebergs produced by this glacier can be enormous, sometimes towering above the water like frozen cliffs.
This glacier is especially important because it has been one of the fastest-moving and most closely studied glaciers in the world. Its speed, thinning, and calving behavior have helped scientists understand how the Greenland Ice Sheet responds to warming air and ocean conditions. The surrounding icefjord is also one of the most visually striking glacier landscapes on Earth, with floating icebergs filling the dark Arctic water.

23. Briksdal Glacier

Type: Outlet Glacier
Location: Vestland, Norway
Status: Retreating
Area: Part of Jostedalsbreen Ice Cap
Elevation: 1,200 m
Temp: -6°C
About
Briksdal Glacier is a famous outlet glacier of the larger Jostedalsbreen ice cap in Norway. It descends between steep mountain walls into the beautiful Briksdalen valley, where waterfalls, green slopes, and cold glacier streams create a dramatic Nordic landscape. The glacier has long been one of Norway’s most visited glacier locations because it is easier to reach than many remote ice formations.
The glacier is also a clear example of how outlet glaciers can change quickly. Its front has moved back and forth over time, but recent retreat has made the ice less accessible than it once was. Even so, Briksdal remains an important location for understanding how a large ice cap feeds smaller glacier arms into valleys. It is especially useful in an educational app because it connects glacier science with visible landscape change.

24. Nigardsbreen Glacier

Location: Jostedal, Norway
Status: Retreating / Fluctuating
Area: Part of Jostedalsbreen Ice Cap
Elevation: 1,500 m
Temp: -7°C
About
Nigardsbreen is another well-known outlet glacier of Jostedalsbreen, the largest ice cap in mainland Europe. It flows down from the high ice plateau into a valley with a cold glacial lake at its front. The glacier is famous for its blue ice, crevasses, and strong visual contrast between white ice, dark rock, and green Norwegian valley landscapes.
This glacier is often used for guided glacier walks, but it is also a place where safety is very important. The ice front can be unstable, and falling ice or sudden changes near the glacier edge can be dangerous. For an educational app, Nigardsbreen is a strong example of how beautiful glacier landscapes require respect and careful observation. It can also help users understand glacier meltwater, proglacial lakes, and seasonal changes in ice shape.

26. Malaspina Glacier
Coordinates: 59.9167, -140.5000
Type: Piedmont Glacier
Location: Alaska, USA
Status: Retreating / Thinning
Area: 3,900 km²
Elevation: 600 m
Temp: -8°C
About
Malaspina Glacier is one of the largest piedmont glaciers in the world. It spreads outward at the base of the Saint Elias Mountains, forming a huge fan-shaped ice mass near the Gulf of Alaska. Unlike narrow valley glaciers that stay between mountain walls, Malaspina flows out onto flatter land, creating a broad and dramatic ice landscape that is especially recognizable from satellite images.
The glacier is partly covered by debris, which gives it dark curved patterns and makes it look very different from clean blue-white ice glaciers. These debris lines show how the glacier has carried rock material from the mountains and spread it across the ice surface. Malaspina is an excellent example for teaching glacier types because its wide, rounded form clearly shows what a piedmont glacier is. It also connects glacier science with remote sensing, since much of its structure is best viewed from above.
Key Facts
One of the world’s largest piedmont glaciers
Spreads out in a wide fan shape near the Gulf of Alaska
Flows from the Saint Elias Mountains
Famous for its dramatic appearance in satellite images
Partly covered by rock debris and dark moraine patterns
Important example of a glacier spreading onto flatter land
Shows how glaciers transport rock material over long distances
Remote and difficult to explore on foot
Useful for explaining glacier morphology and ice movement
Covers a very large lowland ice area near the coast
